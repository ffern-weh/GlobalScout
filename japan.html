Places to Visit

Tokyo
A dazzling metropolis where futuristic innovation meets ancient tradition.
Kyoto
A timeless city where serene temples and vibrant geishas paint a picture of Japan's rich cultural heritage.
Osaka
A lively blend of culinary delights and vibrant nightlife.
Nara
Ancient temples and roaming deer in a tranquil, historic setting.
Okinawa
Known for its tropical climate, beautiful beaches, and coral reefs.
Hiroshima
Known for its Peace Memorial Park, which commemorates the 1945 atomic bombing.

Must try food

Gyoza
Pan-fried dumplings filled with meat and vegetables.
Onigiri
Rice balls filled with fish or pickled ingredients.
Soba
Buckwheat noodles, served chilled or in a hot broth.
Sushi
Iconic Japanese dish of vinegared rice with diverse toppings.
Tempura
Lightly battered, deep-fried seafood or vegetables.
Udon
Thick wheat noodles in broth, simple and satisfying.
Takoyaki
Ball-shaped, filled with octopus, a street food favourite.

Hotels

HotelLeo Yu Capsule
A reasonably priced capsule hotel located , only 3 minutes on foot from the JR Funabashi Station,Tokyo.
Hotel Yu Shu Osaka
A traditional Japanese inn offering a peaceful retreat.
Hotel New Wakasa Nara
A resort with beautiful natural surroundings and hot springs.
Kyoto Takasegawa Bettei
A quiet spot in Kyoto within walking distance from the train station, shops and restaurants, this ryokan is a gem.
Ryukyu Onsen Okinawa
Ryukyu Onsen Senagajima Hotel features an outdoor pool, a hot spring bath and massage treatments.
Nest Hotel Hiroshima
Nesthotel Hiroshima Station is a modern and stylish hotel conveniently located in the heart of Hiroshima, Japan.

Must do things in Japan

Arashiyama
Arashiyama is a small town on the Oi River. Its moon-crossing bridge, Togetsu-kyo, is a beautiful, wooden structure.
Visit teamLab Planets
A museum where you walk through water, and a garden where you become one with the flowers.
Dotombori District
The heart of Osaka nightlife, this area is filled with theaters, restaurants, cafes, bars, arcades and nightclubs.
Hiroshima Museum
This park commemorates the explosion of the first atomic bomb, and houses the Peace Memorial Museum and monuments.
Samurai Experience
Try your hand at being a samurai for the day during an experience that’s designed with families and first-time visitors in mind.
Shibuya Go Kart
You’ll drive your own go-kart on the roads through some of the busiest parts of the central city.
